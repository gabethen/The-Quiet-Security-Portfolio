# Mission 01 – Thinking Like a Security Analyst

## Objective

Learn how security analysts investigate incidents without making assumptions.

---

## What I Learned

Every failed login attempt is not necessarily a hacker.

A security analyst should stay calm, gather evidence, and avoid assumptions.

The investigation should always begin by asking five logical questions.

---

## The Five Questions

### 1. What happened?

A user attempted to log in from two different locations.

### 2. When did it happen?

- 9:00 AM – New York
- 9:20 AM – London

### 3. Who was involved?

A user account.

### 4. Is this normal?

No.

The logins occurred from two distant locations within a short period of time, making the activity suspicious.

### 5. What should happen next?

- Monitor the account
- Reset the password
- Notify the user
- Escalate if necessary

---

## Reflection

This mission taught me that cybersecurity is about investigating evidence rather than jumping to conclusions. Following a structured process helps ensure decisions are based on facts.
